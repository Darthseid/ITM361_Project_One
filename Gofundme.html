An Eyedea:Ending blindness

$51,000 goal

Campaign created 12 months ago
Donate Now  Share on Facebook
 Share
  Tweet
 4 shares
  • Story
  • Updates   1
Jared Mcvey  NAPERVILLE, IL
My arms and legs work perfectly fine, however, I have astigmatism so I wear glasses all the time. Also, my grandfather has to walk with a cane, uses a hearing aid, and recently got a hip replacement, and my stepbrother has type 1 diabetes. When I viewed films like Robocop, were a man who died was rebuilt, I realized the power of technology to provide needs! Then, I saw news stories and articles of wounded soldiers given mechanical limbs and babies without legs being able to walk, that solidified my interest in the possible potential for prosthetics. It doesnt have to be limited to handicapped people either, but anyone with organ or health issues like my family.

To bridge the gap between science fiction and reality, I was originally planning on becoming a mechanical engineer when I entered high school because mechanical prosthetics would work better in poorer conditions/countries, but after personal research, biomedical engineering seemed closer to the cutting edge of bionics and more related to my dream. After having conversations with BME graduates though, I made my final change to electrical engineering since it seemed easier to get into and more design oriented. BME is too broad of a field and EE and MEs are the one who actually create the original prosthetics.





Specifically, I want to design bionic organs, like eyes. There is an apparatus or prosthetic for arms, legs, loss of hearing, etc. but not one for blindness. I am preparing by taking physics, engineering courses, and anatomy courses, so I can make eyes that not only restore vision but can possibly enhance it with a heads up display or zoom. With college tuition debt off my shoulders, I can devote my whole life to designing eye prosthesis.  Professor Troyk, chair of the IIT Biomedical engineering department has even helped me focused my dream by sponsoring me and creating prototype models. 



I dream of meeting an elderly, disabled man or woman who has been blind his or her whole life, and giving them a new experience with the gift of sight.
